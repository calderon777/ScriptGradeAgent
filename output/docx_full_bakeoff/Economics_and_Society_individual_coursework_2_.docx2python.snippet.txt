Q1:

Private school payoff for each family:

<latex>ULpriv=PL+10yty-(1+τ)t</latex><latex>UMpriv=PM+10yty-(1+τ)t</latex><latex>UHpriv=PH+10yty-(1+τ)t</latex>

--	<latex>Pi</latex>: private value by income type (captures that higher-income families may value private schooling more: <latex>PH>PM>PL</latex>). This shows that higher-income households are more willing to pay.

--	<latex>10yty</latex>: is the effect from private sector scale <latex>F=yt</latex>. It rises with total fee income but is inversely proportional to private pupils. This captures that private schooling quality/benefits depend partly on resources and composition.

--	<latex>-(1+τ)t</latex>: total cost paid by families (tuition + VAT). Raising τ increases the private price and can push some households toward public schooling.

--	Public payoff includes <latex>5T</latex>: VAT is recycled into public schools with a multiplier of 5, indicating that an additional amount of money can increase the quality of public schools (policy benefit) and introduces a redistribution between the payers to the private schools and the users in the public one.
