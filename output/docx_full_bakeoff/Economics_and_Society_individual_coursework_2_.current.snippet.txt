Q1:
Private school payoff for each family:
ULpriv=PL+10yty-(1+τ)tUMpriv=PM+10yty-(1+τ)tUHpriv=PH+10yty-(1+τ)t
Pi: private value by income type (captures that higher-income families may value private schooling more: PH>PM>PL). This shows that higher-income households are more willing to pay.
10yty: is the effect from private sector scale F=yt. It rises with total fee income but is inversely proportional to private pupils. This captures that private schooling quality/benefits depend partly on resources and composition.
-(1+τ)t: total cost paid by families (tuition + VAT). Raising τ increases the private price and can push some households toward public schooling.
Public payoff includes 5T: VAT is recycled into public schools with a multiplier of 5, indicating that an additional amount of money can increase the quality of public schools (policy benefit) and introduces a redistribution between the payers to the private schools and the users in the public one.

Q4:
Scenario 1: only L public, M & H private ⇒ x=1,y=2,T=2τt
Scenario 2: L & M public, H private ⇒ x=2,y=1,T=τt
Scenario 1 has more VAT revenue (y higher) and less congestion in public (x lower), so public payoff can be high for the single public pupil; but private becomes attractive for M if PMis high and costs aren’t too high.
Increase in S (baseline public quality): makes public more attractive for everyone therefore pushes toward Scenario 2 (more public).
Increase in τ (VAT rate): raises private cost (1+τ)t which makes private less attractive which pushes toward Scenario 2. It also raises public funding via T which can strengthen public attractiveness too (again toward Scenario 2). Overall higher τ makes Scenario 2 more likely (more public attendance).
Increase in t (tuition): increases private cost directly also affects the “private benefit” term 10t/y, but the dominant effect is typically higher cost which pushes toward Scenario 2.
Increase in PMor  PH(taste/valuation for private): makes private more attractive for those types which pushes toward Scenario 1 (more private).
